  • I would like it to be known that this was the height of Zoom University. Undoubtedly my least favorite time to be a student.
  • ALA 100 - Intro to Environmental Design

    • Course Description

    "Surveys environmental design: includes historic examples and the theoretical, social, technical, and environmental forces that shape them."

    ALA 100 had students understand the importance of desigining a product with many factors at play. Social, technical, and environmental factors can influence how you design a product. The class was online and had weekly video lectures that were accompanied by small quizzes. The bulk of the course came in the form of one major project that was split into phases. I can't remember the specifics, but I do know that we essentially had to document our process of finding what generally makes a good chair and then go on to create our own. It was an okay class. Not my favorite.

  • CHM 116 - General Chemistry II

    • Course Description

    "The main topics covered in CHM116 are chemical kinetics, chemical equilibrium, acid-base equilibria, thermodynamics, electrochemistry and nuclear chemistry."

    Again... just. No. Chemistry is so difficult and for what? This was a BEGINNER course and I was struggling immensely. 0/10 would take again.

  • CSE 120 - Digital Design Fundamentals

    • Course Description

    "Students will be able to analyze, design, construct, and debug digital combinational logic circuitry and digital finite state machine circuitry."

    WE'RE BACK! More computer science material. This class gave students a look at the low level workings of classical computers. "Combinational logic circuitry" is a fancy phrase for combining logic gates to create a circuit that will handle bit operations. I learned things like how to manually work with binary and other bases like hexadecimal, understand structures like multiplexers and registers, and what finite state machines are. Looking back, it was a pretty involved course for a 100 level course but it was still very interesting. I enjoyed it.

  • CSE 259 - Logic in Computer Science

    • Course Description

    "This course provides a mathematically solid introduction to propositional logic, first order logic, logic programming, and their applications in computer science and other fields."

    More Logic! Content-wise, incredible material. It was mainly theoretical but it helped to fortify my knowledge of truth tables, truth statements, and working with boolean logic. This class is the computer science side of discrete mathematics. We started with propositional logic which involved taking declarative sentences, breaking them down into symbols, adding connectives, and then creating a formula to create the same proposition. Once you have the formula, you can manipulate it to create different propositions. After understanding this, we moved into first-order logic and then into logic programming. We used Prolog to do our programming.

  • MAT 265 - Calculus for Engineers I

    • Course Description

    "In this course, we will focus on limits and continuity, differential calculus of functions of one variable, and an introduction to integration."

    Here we go again. "MAT 265 - Calculus for Engineers I" is a required course for computer science majors so I have no choice but to retake this class. Long story short: I failed again and I had to request a third time repeat which wasn't fun but if I want to graduate with this degree, I have to get past this class. And trust me when I say I will!
