  • CSE 301 - Computing Ethics

    • Course Description

    Ethics for computing majors: history of computing, intellectual property, privacy, ethical frameworks, professional ethical responsibilities, and risks of computer-based systems.

    CSE 301 is a necessary course for all computer science majors and for good reason. This class highlighted the importance of understanding that, as an engineer, you are responsible for any systems that you create that the public will use. In the same breath, discussing the importance of respecting the profession and being generally respectful. If you're work seems dangerous or you see work being done that could be dangerous, speak up even if that means you could lose your job. The class mainly consisted of texbook readings with comprehension quizzes and a few papers to write. The papers were more personal rather than research-based.

  • CSE 360 - Intro to Software Engineering

    • Course Description

    "This course is an overview of the “Introduction to Software Engineering” course. It will cover the following topics:
    • Software lifecycle models
    • Software development methods and tools
    • Unified Modeling Language
    • Software project management
    • Software testing
    • Software quality assurance
    • Introduction to information management"

    CSE 360 was a great class for me. The course had a couple of weekly lectures that went over the process of creating software. The class was broken into groups of four-to-five students and a main project was given out which was the bulk of the course. Students were tasked with creating an application with Java.

    Our group decided to create an application that serves as a restaurant's ordering platform. We had monthly project updates as well as weekly group meeting updates. Basically a "what did you do this week" update. This class was fun for me because I just love programming and creating a product. It was also very engaging, having to interact with a group all the time which I am assuming is what the industry will be like.

  • CSE 365 - Information Assurance

    • Course Description

    "Concepts of information assurance (IA); basic IA techniques, policies, risk management, administration, legal and ethics issues."

    Information Assurance is similar to cybersecurity. Where cybersecurity focuses on networks and devices, information assurance focuses on data. This was a greast class that was incredibly challenging for me personally. Concepts were complex and assignments took some time if you had little to no experience in Python (like me). The course went over topics like types of security protocols, cryptography, network/web/system security, and legal and ethical issues. The course used a capture the flag (CTF) framework for assignments which is a common learning style, I hear, for this sort of material. My professor for this class was Adam Doupé. He was amazing. Very knowledgeable and has plenty of experience so he was quite the help for the course.

  • MAT 243 - Discrete Math Structures

    • Course Description

    "Logic, sets, functions, elementary number theory and combinatorics, recursive algorithms, and mathematical reasoning, including induction. Emphasizes connections to computer science."

    MAT 243 introduced me to a ton of important computer science ideas. Discrete math went over proofs, functions, algorithms and their complexities, and integer representation which are all important ideas. Most of the material was confusing for me mainly because the material was so theoretical. Later I will realize why but, at the moment, it was next to useless for me.

  • GCU 421 - Geography Arizona/SW U.S.

    • Course Description

    "Geography of the Southwest with an emphasis on Arizona. Divided into physical geography, history, people, and economy."

    I needed just one more class to keep my scholarships for the semester so I ended up with this class. I mainly took it because it was the only one that seemed interesting to me in the massive list of classes I could take. It was interesting but tedious like the geology course last semester. Homework every few days that corresponded with lengthy lectures and readings from a book. I will say that I learned a lot though. Living in Arizona for my entire life but not knowing much except for the major Phoenix area is not something I am proud of.

  • This was a good semester for me! Mentally challenging with interesting topics. On to the next semester!
