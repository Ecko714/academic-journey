Plans For The Future

Whenever I am with family, or really talking to anyone who is interested in my education, a lot of the time the conversation culminates into the same question: "So. What do you want to do with your degree?". Usually people have a hard time answering this question but I've had to explain it so many times that I think I've got it down well. Here's what I say everytime: My goal, as a programmer, coder, developer, [insert X computer-science-y sounding job title], is to work in a really cool office, with a lively team, and work on projects all day. It sounds so vague and non-compelling but for me, it's the kind of work that I really like. Whenever I get the chance to sit down, put my headphones on, and just code for hours on end, I really am having the best time. At the moment, I'm not getting paid for it... eventually, I will.

What Are We Doing Here?

This section isn't meant to throw you into an existential crisis, sorry about that. I just wanted to give a quick introduction into the rest of the website and give insight on the creation. As a First Generation student, I don't exactly have a role-model to look upon for guidance on what I should be doing or how I should be doing things. Thus, I have resorted to the internet to guide me in the right direction. However, I found that a lot of resources never gave a look into what I would actually be learning. Most anecdotal resources, as well as official, often gave a title and a small description of what classes were; which is not very helpful when you are starting your computer science education with a very limited background. So, here I am, with a complete breakdown of my computer science degree so far! I will be discussing terms and concepts that I've learned so far so be ready for some technical jargon. You can use the tabs on the left side to view any semesters of mine you are interested in!
