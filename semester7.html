  • CSE 340 - Principles of Programming Languages

    • Course Description

    "Students will:
    • understand the basics of recursive descent parsing.
    • Students will describe program execution using box and circle diagrams.
    • Students will understand static scoping.
    • Students will understand parameter-passing mechanisms and their implementation.
    • Students will understand the advantage of object-oriented design and behavior.
    • Students will think functionally when creating functional programs.
    • Students will think declaratively when creating declarative programs"

    Uh Oh. Time to start the trifecta. The trifecta is what students call the "weeder" classes. Anecdotally, they exist to see if students are willing to put in the time to show that they are capable enough to call themselves "computer scientists".

    CSE 340 was my introduction to things like language analysis and program functionality. We start with lexical analysis and move our way upward towards type checking and creating our own compiler. We dip our toes into lambda calculus but we didn't have any assignments on it or anything, just on the final. The class was interesting at best and exhuasting at worst.

  • CSE 355 - Intro to Theoretical Computer Science

    • Course Description

    "Introduces formal language theory and automata, Turing machines, decidability/undecidability, recursive function theory, and complexity theory."

    Another piece of the trifecta! Thankfully, this one I enjoyed. However, it was still incredibly difficult for me. The material was absolutely tricky and convoluted near the end of the semester. We start with a bit of the same material from CSE 340: understanding how languages work. We then move into automata which are computational models consisting of states, transitions, and transition functions. After that we dive deeper into theory relating to decidability and thus, undecidability. Essentially understanding the scope of problems that classical computers can solve.

    This was also another class where I had a great professor. Professor Lee was incredibly engaging and wanted his students to succeed at grasping this sort of material. Again, although incredibly difficult, it was still a great class for me.

  • CSE 463 - Intro to Human Computer Interaction

    • Course Description

    "Design, evaluate, and implement interactive software intended for human use."

    CSE 463 have students understand how to find a problem, brainstorm ideas, and find a solution all while keeping several factors in mind including, but not limited to: human capability, design laws, and public needs. I enjoyed this class because of how involved it was. The lecture component wasn't my favorite but my instructor seemed knowledgeable so it wasn't so bad. But myself and the group that I was paired with actually went through the entire design process for an application. We performed analyses on our idea and createed prototypes and even tested our prototypes on a small group of individuals.

  • MAT 266 - Calculus for Engineers II

    • Course Description

    "Methods of integration, applications of calculus, elements of analytic geometry, improper integrals, Taylor series."

    I retook this class as a session A course so that way I could get it over with while my knowledge was still fresh from the last semester. It went well! I passed with a B! There isn't much else I need to say about this course except for another shout out to Professor Inozemtseva. She's the best.

  • And that is my computer science degree so far! Kinda cool, huh? I've had a great time and I still have a few more semesters to go.
